Making the Decision

But we're not done yet! After our neuron adds up all its weighted inputs, it needs to make a decision – to fire or not to fire. This is where something called an activation function comes in.

Imagine you're at a party, and the noise level is gradually increasing. There's a point where you suddenly decide 'Okay, now it's too loud!' This sudden threshold is similar to how biological neurons work. But in our artificial version, we can use smoother, more flexible decision functions.

Build Your Vocab

Activation Function

A mathematical function that determines the neuron's output based on its weighted sum of inputs. It's like the neuron's decision-making rule.
