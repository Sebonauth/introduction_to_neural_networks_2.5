An artificial neuron, also known as a node or perceptron, simplifies the function of a biological neuron into a mathematical model.

Continue

The main components of an artificial neuron are:

Continue

1. Inputs (\( x_i \)): These represent the data or signals coming into the neuron, similar to dendrites receiving signals.

Continue

2. Weights (\( w_i \)): Each input is multiplied by a weight, which signifies the importance or strength of that input.

Weights: Weights are factors that adjust the input signals, determining their influence on the neuron's output.

Continue

3. Bias (\( b \)): An additional parameter that allows us to shift the activation function, providing greater flexibility.

Continue

4. Summation Function: The neuron computes a weighted sum of its inputs:

\[ z = \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i x_i + b \]

Continue

5. Activation Function (\( \phi(z) \)): This function processes the weighted sum to produce the neuron's output. Common activation functions include the step function, sigmoid, ReLU, and others.

Continue

6. Output (\( y \)): The final output of the neuron after applying the activation function:

\[ y = \phi(z) \]
